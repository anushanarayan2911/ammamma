A Life of Service

“When beauty, talent, intellect, and service know no bounds, they touch millions of lives. Such a soul is not merely the beloved of a family, but a cherished gift to society.”

Dr. Seetha spent almost six decades embodying this philosophy across medical clinics, university classrooms, and her own home.

Our Tribute

Celebrating Her Life

Dr. Seetha Tekumalla was a beacon of light, healing, and absolute dedication. Born in 1950, she pursued medicine at a time when few women of her background did, driven by an insatiable curiosity and a deep, innate desire to alleviate human suffering. She went on to touch thousands of lives as a distinguished professor of medicine.

Her clinic was more than a medical space; it was a sanctuary of safety. Parents from all walks of life remember her gentle voice, her patience in listening, and the way she made every child feel uniquely seen and cared for. In the academic world, her students remember her as a demanding yet incredibly nurturing mentor who taught the art of empathy alongside medical science.

Beyond her stellar career, she was a mother, grandmother, and constant pillar of support for her family. Her home was always filled with the comforting scent of freshly brewed tea, classical music, and endless books. Her legacy survives through her family, her hundreds of grateful patients, and the generation of doctors she inspired to practice medicine with a heart.

Life Timeline

A Journey of Grace & Impact

  1. 1927

    Early Beginnings

    Born to a family of educators, instilling a lifelong devotion to learning, classical arts, and community service.

  2. 1974

    Medical Degree & Specialization

    Graduated top of her medical class with honors, specializing in pediatrics to care for the most vulnerable.

  3. 1982

    Establishing Her Practice

    Opened her family practice clinic, creating a patient-first haven known for accessible and deeply empathetic care.

  4. 1995

    Joining the Faculty

    Appointed Professor of Pediatrics. Over the next 25 years, she trained over a thousand future medical professionals.

  5. 2015

    Lifetime Achievement

    Received the Lifetime Medical Excellence award for her outstanding contributions to pediatric public health.

  6. 2026

    A Peaceful Farewell

    Passed away peacefully in her sleep, leaving behind an indelible blueprint of a life magnificently lived.
